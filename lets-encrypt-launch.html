Let's Encrypt

Launching a free CA

Roland Shoemaker / @rolandshoemaker
Technology Fellow, EFF

What

  • New widely trusted certificate authority
    • FREE!
    • Open source
    • Based on the ACME protocol (currently draft RFC)
    • Golang based backend services and (official) Python client
  • Single command issuance & installation

    sudo letsencrypt run

Who

Originally two EFF and Mozilla projects merged under ISRG (Internet Security Research Group)

Developers from EFF, ISRG, University of Michigan, Mozilla, et al

Sponsors

Why?

Prevalence & privacy/safety implications of HTTP

Issuance and installation complexity

  • Complexity of SSL issuance, installation, and configuration

    • Poor security track record

    Lax CA/B requirements

    Ridiculous number of roots

    Solution

    ACME

    boulder

    Entirely open source CA, licensed under MPLv2, written in Golang, and audited by NCC Group

    letsencrypt

    Official fully automated client with plugins for Apache and nginx that handle installation and certificate life-cycle management

    What we aren't doing

    • Wildcards (until WG decides on validation plan)
    • Longer than 90 day validity
    • EV or OV certificates (due to validation requirements)
